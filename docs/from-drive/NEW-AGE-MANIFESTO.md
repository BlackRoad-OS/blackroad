## The Long History of Computer Frustration (1960s–Today)

### The Command Line Era (1960s–1980s)
- Punch cards requiring perfect formatting
- Terminal commands with cryptic syntax (`chmod 755`, `tar -xvzf`)
- No error recovery — one typo could crash hours of work
- Documentation written by engineers, for engineers
- The "expert culture" that gatekept computing from ordinary people

### The GUI Promise That Never Fully Delivered (1980s–2000s)
- Icons that never explained what they did
- Error messages like "Error 0x80070057" with no human explanation
- Registry editing required for basic customization
- Driver hell — printers, scanners, graphics cards constantly breaking
- The Blue Screen of Death as a cultural trauma

### The Fragmentation Explosion (2000s–2010s)
- Windows vs Mac vs Linux — choose your incompatibility
- Mobile vs desktop — different universes
- Cloud vs local — data scattered everywhere
- Apps that don't talk to each other
- Workflows requiring 5+ apps to accomplish one task
- The rise of "integration specialists" just to connect basic tools

### The Modern Complexity Crisis (2010s–Today)
- Subscription fatigue across 20+ services
- Update notifications interrupting flow states
- Security patches breaking functionality
- Cloud sync conflicts destroying files
- Platform lock-in trapping users and data
- The death of "it just works"

**Real Human Cost:**
- Average knowledge worker switches between 10 apps 25 times per day
- 20% of work time lost to context switching
- Millions of hours spent on "computer maintenance" instead of creation
- Children learning to fear technology instead of play with it

## Legacy Enterprise Systems That Failed Users

### The JDE/SAP/PeopleSoft Nightmare
- Launching JDE feels like booting Windows 95 in 2025
- 47 clicks to approve a purchase order
- Training takes 6 months; mastery takes years
- Forms designed by people who've never used forms
- Drop-down menus with 500 unsorted options
- Green-screen interfaces in a 4K world

**The Financial Toll:**
- SAP implementations average $500K–$5M for mid-size companies
- 55–75% of SAP projects go over budget
- Hidden costs in training, customization, and consultant fees
